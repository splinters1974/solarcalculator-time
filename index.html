⚡ Large System — NESO Involvement Required
This system exceeds 5MWp, which means the grid connection application moves beyond your local Distribution Network Operator (DNO) and involves the National Energy System Operator (NESO). This significantly extends the grid approval timeline — typically an additional 6 months or more. The timeline below reflects this. Factor this into any funding or procurement decisions.
What is the DNO / NESO? The DNO (Distribution Network Operator) is your local electricity network company — they approve and manage how your solar system connects to the grid. For systems over 5MWp, responsibility escalates to the NESO (National Energy System Operator), which coordinates connections at a national infrastructure level. Both approvals are required before your system can export or operate at full capacity. DNO approval typically takes around 12 weeks; NESO involvement adds a further 6 months or more.
